# GTM evidence — real-world signal ledger

> Owned by the GTM Director (`/SDLC-gtm` orchestrator). The **hard rule** (see `memory/standards.md` GTM
> evidence): every estimate, projection, valuation, demand, or pricing claim must cite **≥1 non-zero
> real-world signal** recorded here, and must state the evidence it rests on. No row = no number;
> the honest answer is "we don't know yet — here's the cheapest test."
>
> A "signal" is something that actually happened in the world: ad impressions/clicks/cost-per-click,
> landing-page visits/conversions, waitlist sign-ups, user-test results. **Not** an assumption, a
> comparable-company figure, or a model guess.

## Signals

| Date | Source (test/channel) | Metric | Result | Cost | Notes (incl. net-new angle) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| _e.g._ 2026-06-07 | $20 IG ad → landing | clicks / signups | 240 clicks · 18 signups (7.5%) | $19.40 | first paid test; angle: "calm focus" |

## Claims registry (every estimate must point at a row above)

| Claim / estimate | Rests on (signal rows) | Confidence | Stated by |
|---|---|---|---|
| _e.g._ "~7% landing conversion" | 2026-06-07 ad test | low (n=240, one channel) | gtm-strategist |

> If a needed claim has no backing row, it stays in the strategist's report as a **hypothesis-to-test**
> with the cheapest test named — it does **not** get stated as a finding.
